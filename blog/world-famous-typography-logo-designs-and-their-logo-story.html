World Famous Typography Logo Designs and Their Logo Story

Logo Design

By Contributor    on Mar 21, 2023

    Just imagine you've got the most distinctive, eye-catching logo design on the planet. Everyone sees your logo and tells you that their friends thought it was cool, they loved it, and they couldn't believe how much impact your logo made. 

    Here's an open secret - there is a lot more than meets the eye in achieving a great logo design.

    There are companies with popular typography logos that everyone knows—even people who don't work in design. They aren't just the best logo designers of our time (each one of them!). 

    In this post, we'll show you some of the best typography logo designs and their logo story.

    Amazing Typography Logo Designs and Their Logo Story

    Can you imagine a world without typography? Typography is everywhere — on the street signs, in newspapers, in magazines, and even in TV commercials.

    The best part about typography is that it can be used to convey different messages, depending on how you want to use it. A logo can be designed using different fonts and colors. When designing the perfect logo for your business, you need to consider various factors such as color scheme, font choice, and image usage.

    The best way to get started with creating a great logo is by looking at some of the best examples out there. Here are some amazing typography logo designs that will inspire you:

    Amazon 

    The Amazon typography and lettering for logo design is a great example of typography as design. The arrow shape of the letter “a” is visually striking and memorable. This is one of the most successful logos of all time, so there’s no wonder why it has been copied so many times.

    Google

    The Google logo is another great example of typography as a design. It’s simple and memorable, but it also has an interesting backstory. According to Sergey Brin, who co-founded Google with Larry Page, the Google logo was inspired by the mathematical symbol for googol, which is 10100 (or 10 raised to the 100th power). A googol has nearly as many zeros as there are atoms in our solar system!

    LinkedIn

    LinkedIn is one of the most popular social networking sites in the world today. It was founded by Reid Hoffman in 2002 as an online business card exchange service for Harvard alumni. It has now become an international enterprise that provides job listings, company profiles, industry news, and educational material on its site.

    Canon 

    The Canon logo is one of the most famous logos in the world. It is also one of the oldest, as it was introduced in 1934. The giant red “C” has been used for over 80 years now, and it has become a symbol of Japanese technology. The company has been using creative typography logo designs to represent its high-quality cameras, printers, and scanners ever since.

    Coca-Cola

    The Coca-Cola logo was created by Frank Mason Robinson in 1885, and it didn’t change until 1986 when it got its current form. However, this classic design remains one of the most recognizable logos in the world. It is simple, yet stylish and unique at the same time. That’s why thousands of designers are trying to create their versions of this logo every year!

    Adidas

    Adidas is the name of a German company that makes athletic shoes, clothing, and accessories. The company was founded in 1924 by Adolf Dassler, who joined forces with his brother Rudolf to create a new shoe manufacturing company called GebrüderDasslerSchuhfabrik (Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory). Rudolf had been employed at a shoe manufacturing plant in Herzogenaurach called Ruda, which later became Puma. The brothers split in 1948 after an intense argument over money and control of the business. Adolf then changed the name of his company to Adidas.

    The typography and lettering for the logo design symbolize the three stripes on all Adidas products as well as "the movement of athletes."

    Disney

    Disney The logo of this popular brand was created by the Disney brothers in 1928. It is a simple yet powerful design. The logo consists of two circles that represent Mickey Mouse's head and body. The two circles are joined together to form a heart shape, which is the symbol of love and affection. The creative typography logo design has undergone a few changes over the years, but the core concept remains the same.

    FedEx 

    FedEx is a common name, but it's not just a company. It's an abbreviation of Federal Express Corporation. The company was formed in 1971 by Frederick W. Smith, who saw the need for overnight delivery of packages and documents. He started the company in Little Rock, Arkansas with only $4 million and a leased aircraft fleet of three 727 jets. Today, FedEx has over 70,000 trucks and planes that serve 220 countries and territories worldwide..

    Conclusion

    As you could see from this latest logo collection, design companies and their clients highly value typographical designs. Creative and meaningful typography logos are noticed quickly, and they're remembered longer. Think about the creative typography logo designs of any brand. Chances are that its name contains some type of wordplay or famous quotes.
