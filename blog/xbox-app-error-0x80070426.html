What is Xbox App Error 0x80070426- How to Fix?

General

By John Fernandes    modified Oct 16, 2025

~ 5 minutes to read

    Most gaming maniacs spend the best of their time as Xbox geeks get frustrated quickly. It is because the gaming app often comes across a series of errors that can hinder one’s overall experience. One of the most frustrating and popular errors encountered by this platform is the Xbox App Error 0x80070426.  

    According to customer feedback, this error mostly occurs when users are trying to sign in to the gaming app.  Because this error prevents gamers from accessing their games or utilizing any other features, experts have successfully found a few solutions to resolve it.  
    Getting into the shoes of all Xbox gamers, we have shared a detailed log explaining the Xbox App Error 0x80070426 along with the necessary fixtures that may help resolve it quickly.

    What is Xbox App Error 0x80070426?

    The Xbox App Error 0x80070426 often occurs when Windows PC users are trying to access Microsoft account services.  Whenever the Windows Store comes across any issues and users are unable to access their Microsoft accounts, this error appears.  Also,  a few reasons behind the occurrence of this error include misconfigured network settings, corrupted system files, or Windows Update service failures.

    As soon as this error occurs, players are unable to access the Xbox game pass and Xbox Live along with any other features.

    Common Causes of Xbox App Error 0x80070426

    Here are a few prominent reasons that may cause the Xbox App Error 0x80070426.

    Windows Store Account Issues

    Users may encounter this error when the Windows Store on your PC is unable to recognize your Microsoft account credentials.

    Out-Of-Date Windows Operating System

    If you have not updated your Windows operating system to the latest version, you may experience compatibility issues with the gaming app.

    Corrupted System Files

    In case any of the system files on your PC or corrupted, your gaming app may not be able to function properly and cause the Error 0x80070426.

    Network Configuration Problems

    If your network settings are not accurate,  your gaming app will not be able to create a seamless connection with Microsoft services.

    Windows Update Service Issues

    Because gaming apps only rely on up-to-date system files, keeping your Windows operating system updated to the latest version is necessary, otherwise you may encounter several problems like the error 0X80070426.

    How to Fix Xbox App Error 0x80070426?

    Follow the detailed steps discussed below to fix the Xbox App Error.

    Refresh Your Xbox App Account

    The first fixture according to experts for fixing this type of error is to sign out of your gaming app and sign in again.

    1.    Launch the Xbox app.
    2.    In the top right corner of the app, locate your profile picture and click on it.
    3.    Now select the option “Sign out.”
    4.    Once you have signed out successfully, you click on the option “Sign in.”
    5.    Enter your Microsoft account credentials and sign in again.

    Check for Windows Updates

    Try to check your Windows operating system for any updates as it will help the gaming app to function properly.

    1.    Go to the settings of your Windows PC.
    2.    Locate the option “Update & Security” and click on it.
    3.    Click on the option “Windows Update.”
    4.    Now select the option “Check for updates.”
    5.    In case any updates are available, download and install them.
    6.    Once your Windows is updated to the latest version, restart your system and sign back into the Xbox app again.

    Reset the Xbox App

    By resetting the Xbox gaming app, you may be able to clear any kind of corrupted data that may be causing the Error 0x80070426.

    1.    Go to the settings of your Windows PC
    2.    Locate the option “Apps” and click on it.
    3.    Now select the option “Apps and Features.”
    4.    Scroll down the list to find the Xbox app.
    5.    Click on the name of the gaming app and select the option “Advanced Options.”
    6.    Under the menu of advanced options, scroll down to select the option reset.

    Troubleshoot your Windows Store

    The Microsoft Windows store consists of a build and troubleshooter that can help detect any kind of errors and fix them automatically.

    1.    Go to the settings of your Windows PC.
    2.    Locate the option “Update & Security” and click on it.
    3.    Click on the option “Troubleshoot.”
    4.    Now click on the option “Additional Troubleshooters.”
    5.    From the drop-down menu, scroll down to find the option “Windows Store Apps.”
    6.    Now click on the option “Run the Troubleshooter.”

    Re-register Microsoft Store and Xbox App

    Experts often recommend re-registering your Xbox App and Microsoft Store to rectify any kind of account authentication errors and resolve them. 

    1.    In the Windows start menu, click on the search bar and write “PowerShell.”
    2.    Right-click on PowerShell and select the option “Run as Administrator.”
    3.    Once the PowerShell window opens, copy and paste the following code one by one and press Enter. 

    Code 01:

    Get-AppxPackage -allusers Microsoft.WindowsStore | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register "$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml"}

    Code 02:

    Get-AppxPackage -allusers Microsoft.XboxApp | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register "$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml"}

    4.    Now restart your computer, and log into your Xbox app again using your Microsoft account credentials.

    Create a New User Account

    According to Xbox players, sometimes the Xbox App Error 0x80070426 also appears due to issues with the user profile.  For this reason, you can try to create a new user account and check whether the problem is profile-specific or not.

    1.    Go to settings on your Windows PC.
    2.    Locate the “Accounts” option and click on it.
    3.    Click on the option “Family & other users.”
    4.    Now select the option “Add someone else to this PC.”
    5.    Read the on-screen instructions carefully and create a new user account.
    6.    Now log out of your current account.
    7.    Use your new credentials to log in to the new account.
    8.    Launch the Xbox app and sign in again.

    Check and Repair System Files

    Another prominent cause of Error 0x80070426 is corrupted system files.  You can take the help of different tools like the System File Checker (SFC) or Deployment Imaging Service and Management Tool (DISM) to repair these files.

    1.    In the Windows start menu, click on the search bar and write “CMD.”
    2.    Right-click on Command Prompt and select the option “Run as Administrator.”
    3.    Now write down the following command for SFC and press enter.

    Code 01:

    Sfc /scannow

    4.    Wait for the scan to complete and check whether any corrupted files are found or not. In case of any corrupted files, the system will repair them automatically.
    5.    Now copy and paste the following command for DISM.

    Code 02:

    DISM /Online /Cleanup-Image /RestoreHealth

    6.    Wait for the scan to complete and restart your Windows PC.
    7.    Now sign in to the Xbox app again.

    Reinstall the Xbox App. 

    In case none of the above solutions help out, try to reinstall the Xbox app.

    1.    Go to the settings of your Windows PC
    2.    Locate the option “Apps” and click on it.
    3.    Now select the option “Apps and Features.”
    4.    Scroll down the list to find the Xbox app.
    5.    Click on the name of the gaming app and select the option “Uninstall.”
    6.    Now launch the Microsoft App Store and search for the Xbox app to install it again.

    The Takeaway

    Experiencing the Xbox App Error 0x80070426 continuously can be frustrating for gamers. It wastes your precious time and keeps you from accessing your favorite games. Interestingly, the above-discussed solutions have proven to be an ideal we to remove these errors and make the most of your time for entertainment and gaming. 

    Have a look at each of the solutions above and try them to resolve your Xbox App Error. Share your experience with each of these solutions in the comments below. Also, discuss any other solutions that may help to resolve this error. Keep streaming Xbox seamlessly and enjoy gaming!
